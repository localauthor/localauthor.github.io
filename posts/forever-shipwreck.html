In Itself and Forever Shipwreck

                                            A photograph is not a record of a thing itself

                              but a record of a thing in a moment, as it won't be in the future.

The photograph---the record---will accrue value in direct relation to the nature and extent of
                                                    the changes that will be undergone by the thing photographed.
Or even just the things around it.

One cannot know, then, the value of a photograph in the moment it is taken.

There is no sense in taking photographs of what won’t change:

Photograph the fleeting.

But then, what isn’t fleeting?

Photograph everything.

Every thing.
